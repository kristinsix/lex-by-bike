Bicycle Network in Lexington, KY: Existing network and proposed complementary network along active rail lines


Existing Bicycle Network

Click here for full screen version

Lexington's current bicycle network includes off-road, shared use trails free from automobile traffic, depicted as green lines in the above map. Also included are many on-road bike lanes and sharrows, where cyclists ride mere feet from (or sometimes sharing a lane with) motorists. These on-road facilities are depicted in the above map as red lines: the brighter red indicates a dedicated bike lane*, while the darker red indicates a cyclist must share a lane with motorists or in some cases must ride on a road shoulder that does not meet the standards required for a bike lane.

The above map also includes the streets overlying the bicycle network, depicted as black lines. Line thickness increases as speed limit (and therefore danger to cyclists) increases.

* Dedicated bike lanes include both standard bike lanes and "buffered bike lanes" in which the road is striped with a "buffer" space between vehicles and bikes. Neither case offers a physical barrier between bikes and cars.

Proposed Complementary Network

Link to full-screen version

Shown here in green are the current (existing and funded) off-road shared use trails. Shown in blue are my propsed trails, running alongside active rail lines, showing increased connectivity, including:

  • south side neighborhoods between Nicholasville Rd and Clays Mill Rd, as well as access to retail including the Fayette Mall and Nicholasville Rd corridor
  • east side neighborhoods along Loudon Ave, Winchester Rd, and North Broadway/Bryan Station
  • north side neighborhoods including Meadowthorpe, Georgetown Rd, and the back (eastern portion) of Masterson Station
  • west side neighborhoods including Cardinal Valley
These trails could be extended into neighboring counties to create a network of interconnected cycling communities.
